01 — Care by Species

Tap any animal category below to reveal tailored care instructions, feeding habits, shelter needs, and behavioral tips.

🐶 Dog Care Essentials

  • › Feed high-protein dry or wet food 2–3 times daily based on size and breed
  • › Provide fresh water at all times; clean bowl daily
  • › Exercise daily — min 30 min for small breeds, 90 min for large breeds
  • › Groom weekly; bathe every 4–6 weeks; trim nails monthly
  • › Annual vet visits; semi-annual for dogs over age 7
  • › Deworm every 3 months and maintain flea/tick prevention monthly

🐱 Cat Care Essentials

  • › Feed age-appropriate cat food; avoid dog food or human table scraps
  • › Clean litter box daily; full deep clean weekly
  • › Brush long-haired breeds 2–3 times a week; cats self-groom short coats
  • › Provide vertical space — shelves, cat trees — for climbing and hiding
  • › Spay/neuter to prevent overpopulation and certain health risks
  • › Monitor for dental disease — very common in cats after age 3

🐄 Farm Animal Care Essentials

  • › Provide clean, dry shelter appropriate to species and climate
  • › Cattle need 30–50 liters of fresh water daily; clean troughs regularly
  • › Rotate grazing areas to prevent overgrazing and parasite buildup
  • › Regular hoof trimming for cattle, goats, and horses every 6–8 weeks
  • › Follow government vaccination programs (FMD, LSD, PPR, etc.)

🦜 Avian & Bird Care Essentials

  • › Feed species-appropriate pellets, seeds, and fresh fruits/vegetables
  • › Keep cage out of drafts and direct sunlight; clean weekly
  • › Provide mental stimulation — mirrors, perches, foraging toys
  • › Watch for feather plucking — a sign of stress or nutritional deficiency
  • › Never expose to non-stick cookware fumes — PTFE is lethal to birds

🦁 Wild Animal Encounters

  • › Never attempt to handle or capture wild animals without training
  • › If you find an injured wild animal, contact your local wildlife rescue
  • › Do not feed wild animals — disrupts natural behavior and creates dependency
  • › Keep safe distance: 10m from large mammals, 30m from nesting birds
  • › Any bite — wash wound immediately and seek rabies PEP within 24 hours

🐟 Aquatic Pet Care Essentials

  • › Maintain water temperature, pH, and filtration for your species type
  • › Feed small amounts 1–2 times daily; overfeeding causes ammonia spikes
  • › Perform 25% water changes weekly; test water parameters monthly
  • › Quarantine new fish 2 weeks before introducing to existing tank
  • › Watch for ich, fin rot, and fungal infections; treat promptly

02 — Emergency First Aid

Follow these steps while transporting your animal to emergency veterinary care.

⚠️ Rush to an emergency vet if:

  • › Difficulty breathing or blue gums
  • › Collapse or severe weakness
  • › Suspected poisoning
  • › Deep wounds or eye injury
  1. 1

    Stay calm and restrain gently

    An injured animal may bite. Use a towel to wrap small animals. Muzzle dogs if necessary.

  2. 2

    Control bleeding

    Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth for 5–10 minutes.

  3. 3

    Transport safely

    Use a firm surface for large animals. Minimize movement of fractures.
